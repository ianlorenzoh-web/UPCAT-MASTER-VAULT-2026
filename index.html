🗺️ UPCAT Process Guide

Everything you need to know about the UPCAT — from application to results. Your complete roadmap to UP.

🏛️
University of the Philippines
College Admission Test (UPCAT)

The UPCAT is the college admission test for all UP System campuses. It tests Language Proficiency, Reading Comprehension, Mathematics, and Science. Results are used to compute your University Predicted Grade (UPG).

— Application Process
01
Check Eligibility
Must be a current SHS student graduating in the school year of application, or a recent graduate within 3 years. Check UP Admissions for exact requirements.
02
Online Application
Register at the official UPCAT portal. Fill in personal info, choose campus preferences, and upload required documents (Form 137, ID photo).
03
Application Fee
Pay the application fee at any authorized payment center or online. Fee-waiver options available for qualified low-income applicants (NOA required).
04
Receive Test Permit
Download your UPCAT Test Permit from the portal. This contains your assigned test center, date, and testing room. Print it clearly.
05
Take the UPCAT
4 subtests over 2–3 sessions: Language Proficiency, Reading Comprehension, Mathematics, and Science. 215 items total. Bring permit, valid ID, and pencils.
06
Results & Enrollment
Results released via official UP portal. Passers receive UPG and can apply for enrollment. Waitlisted applicants may be admitted if slots open.
— UPCAT Subtests
📖 Language Proficiency
Vocabulary, grammar, sentence structure, analogies, Filipino language use. Tests both English and Filipino.
📚 Reading Comprehension
Passages with questions on main idea, inference, tone, critical reading. Both English and Filipino texts.
📐 Mathematics
Algebra, geometry, trigonometry, statistics, word problems. Covers K-12 curriculum up to Grade 12.
🧪 Science
Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Earth Science. Covers major concepts from Junior and Senior High School.

— FAQs
Is the UPCAT free to take?
No — there is an application fee, but qualified low-income applicants may apply for a fee waiver. The fee is typically below ₱500. Check the official UP Admissions site for the current fee schedule.
What are the UPCAT exam materials?
You must bring: (1) printed UPCAT Test Permit, (2) at least two valid IDs, (3) pencils (No. 2 or HB), and (4) a pen. Calculators, phones, and electronic devices are strictly prohibited.
How is the UPG calculated?
The University Predicted Grade (UPG) combines your UPCAT subtest scores and your high school GWA. The official formula is not publicly released, but community estimates suggest a roughly 40/60 split between UPCAT percentile rank and high school grades. The lower the UPG, the better.
Can I take the UPCAT more than once?
No. You can only take the UPCAT once. You must be a current SHS student or a recent graduate (within a specific window). This makes thorough preparation extremely important.
When are UPCAT results released?
Results are typically released 4–6 months after the exam, usually around January–March of the following year. Results are announced via the official UP Admissions website and individual campus notices.
